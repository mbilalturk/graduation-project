\begin{center}
\textbf{PREDICTION OF BUS ARRIVAL TIMES IN PUBLIC TRANSPORTATION}
\end{center}

\vspace{0.5cm}

%% Edit below this line
This study presents a context-aware machine learning system for predicting inter-stop bus travel times in the İzmir ESHOT public transportation network. An end-to-end and fully reproducible pipeline was developed that collects real-time bus positions from the city's open transit Application Programming Interface (API), together with static General Transit Feed Specification (GTFS) schedule data and contextual weather and traffic information. From these sources, clean stop-to-stop travel-time segments were extracted and enriched with temporal, spatial, schedule-derived, historical, and dwell-time features. The dataset was collected independently over a period of approximately three months across three bus routes.

A range of models was trained and compared on a common, chronologically split test set: naïve and historical baselines, Linear Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, and a dual-input Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) network. The best models achieve a Mean Absolute Error of about $0.43$ minutes, far below the schedule-based baseline. A rigorous statistical analysis shows that the classical XGBoost model and the deep LSTM are statistically indistinguishable, while both significantly outperform the Random Forest; XGBoost is therefore identified as the most practical single model. The method also generalizes consistently across all three routes.

The project places strong emphasis on methodological honesty through an A/B-driven development process in which each proposed enhancement was retained only if it produced a measurable improvement. This process produced three key findings: the achievable accuracy is bounded by a measurement floor imposed by the 30-second polling interval; reducing model complexity does not reduce accuracy; and a fair same-test-set comparison corrects an earlier impression that the deep model was best. The GTFS schedule-derived and GPS-derived dwell-time features were confirmed to be among the most informative inputs.
